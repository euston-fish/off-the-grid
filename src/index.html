EPISODE II
JS13K STRIKES BACK

In an era of full-time employmenet, oncall time, and travel, two developers attempt to develop an entry for the js13k games competition.

With high hopes they plan an incredible concept - a civilisation-building epic where the player guides a tribe of people through famine, plague, overpopulation. Relaying commandments to their people, the player would be given the role and reponsibility of a God!

And that was not all! The two adventurous developers had a taste for a challenge. The tribe would not be alone in the world.

Other players and their previous incarnations would be fighting for survival alongside them! The interaction between two tribes could not be predicted - would players help or hinder one another?

A game of this scale would not be a simple task.

Our developers were prepared - after experimentation, evaluation, and much hard work a system for simulating the world was created - not with JavaScript, but utilising the power of Web Assembly. This power could process untold amounts of data in mere milliseconds.

However, a common foe of software engineering struck the developers! Time management reigned chaos as the two attempted to balance their work, travel, and social responsibilities with their monumental task of creating a virtual world.
